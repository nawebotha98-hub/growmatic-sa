# Gqeberha (Nelson Mandela Bay) — Local Prospecting Plan

GrowMatic SA's outreach is focused exclusively on businesses based in Gqeberha and the wider Nelson Mandela Bay metro (Despatch, Kariega/Uitenhage, Walmer, Summerstrand, Newton Park, Sunridge Park, Westering, Mount Croix). Local-only focus means shorter sales cycles: you can meet owners face-to-face, demo in person, and referrals travel fast in a tighter business community than in a national campaign.

## Why Gqeberha first

- You can physically visit a prospect within 20 minutes of almost anywhere in the metro — in-person demos close faster than cold video calls.
- Word travels fast in NMB business circles (Rotary, chambers of commerce, local Facebook groups) — 3 happy local clients generates referrals disproportionate to the sample size.
- Less competition for "AI automation" positioning locally than in Cape Town/Joburg, where every agency claims it.

## Target industries in Gqeberha (in priority order)

1. **Real estate agencies** — Summerstrand, Walmer, and the beachfront suburbs have a dense agent market (RE/MAX, Chas Everitt, Pam Golding branches, and independents). High enquiry volume, missed-lead pain is obvious.
2. **Panelbeaters & auto body shops / mechanics** — NMB's automotive manufacturing base (Ford, VW supplier network) means a large ecosystem of independent workshops that miss calls constantly while hands are covered in grease. Ideal Missed Call Text-Back pitch.
3. **Guesthouses & boutique hotels** — Summerstrand and the beachfront have a high concentration of small hospitality operators competing on responsiveness for booking enquiries.
4. **Home services** — electricians, plumbers, security/alarm installers, solar installers (load shedding has made this a growth category in NMB specifically).
5. **Dentists, physios & aesthetic practices** — Newton Park and Walmer medical precincts; booking/no-show pain is concrete and easy to quantify.
6. **Attorneys & accountants** (smaller local firms, not the big nationals) — client intake and admin overload.

## Where to source contacts (all free, all local)

- **Google Maps** — search each industry + "Gqeberha" / "Port Elizabeth" (many local listings still use the old name), scrape business name, phone, website.
- **Facebook groups** — "Port Elizabeth Business Network", "PE/Gqeberha Buy & Sell", local suburb groups (Summerstrand Community, Walmer Residents) often have business owners posting directly.
- **Nelson Mandela Bay Business Chamber** — membership directory; consider joining for credibility + a warm network.
- **Herald / Weekend Post local business listings** and **Yellow Pages ZA** filtered to Gqeberha/PE.
- **Your own network first** — anyone you know in NMB who owns or manages a business, or can introduce you to one. This is still the fastest path to client #1.

## 30-day local plan

**Week 1 — Foundation**
- Build a list of 60 target businesses across the 6 industries above, sourced from Google Maps + Facebook groups.
- Contact your entire personal network in NMB — ask directly, ask for introductions.
- Prepare the 90-second demo video using a real Gqeberha business type as the example (e.g. a Summerstrand guesthouse).

**Week 2 — Outreach**
- Send 15–20 personalised emails/day to the list, referencing something specific and local (their location, a recent Google review, a local event).
- Follow the email sequence in `EMAIL_SCRIPTS.md`, but personalise the opening line with NMB specifics ("I noticed you're right on Beach Road..." / "saw your listing near the Boardwalk...").
- Attend one local networking event or chamber meetup if possible — a face at the table beats a cold email every time in a metro this size.

**Week 3 — Meet in person**
- For anyone who replies, push for an in-person meeting over a video call wherever practical — this is Gqeberha's biggest edge over a national campaign. Bring a laptop, do the demo live.
- Target: 8–10 in-person or video demos.

**Week 4 — Close and get referrals**
- Convert 2–3 pilots.
- Ask every prospect you met (even ones who said no) if they know another NMB business owner who might need this — local referral chains compound fast here.

## Local objection handling additions

- **"We're just a small local business, this is for big companies"** → "This was built for exactly this — a one-person real estate agency in Summerstrand loses the same enquiry to a slow response as a national franchise does. The size of the business doesn't matter; the speed of the reply does."
- **"I'll ask around and see if anyone's used you"** → "Totally fair — I'm just getting started here in Gqeberha, so you'd be among the first. Happy to do the pilot month-to-month so there's no risk while you see how it performs."

## Keeping the plan scoped

Every email script, proposal, and outreach message should reference Gqeberha/Nelson Mandela Bay explicitly — do not send generic "South African business" copy. Prospects respond far better to hyper-local framing than to a broad SA-wide pitch.
